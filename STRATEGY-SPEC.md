## 0. The two jobs of this page (why it exists)

This artifact carries **two conversion jobs at once** — spec both, because they constrain each other.

1. **META-PITCH JOB (primary, right now):** convince **MJ / Ministry Barbers** to hire Automaitions for a full site + bespoke booking app. The buyer is MJ. The page IS the pitch — we show up with the solution already built, not a slide deck.
2. **PROXY JOB (what it demonstrates):** function as Ministry's real homepage would — turn a cold visitor into a **booking, an academy application, or a product sale**. If it converts *their* customers, MJ believes it will convert for him.

**Service vs Product fork:** BOTH are **Service-path** businesses.
- Automaitions → Ministry = service sale, high-touch, human-closed on a call (>$1k, bespoke app). CTA language = "see what's possible / book a call."
- Ministry → its customers = service (chair booking + $5k academy) + a small product line. AOV of the academy ($4–5k) makes even *that* a human-considered, application-style decision.

## 1. Dream Buyer

### 1a. The real buyer (MJ — the meta-pitch)
- Refugee-to-leader founder, faith-driven (Faith · Patience · Success), numbers-aware, brand-obsessed. Built a 553k-follower TikTok personal brand off a single Bass Hill chair.
- **Currently in pain:** no online booking → wait-time complaints + phone tag; unpublished pricing; academy sells out on a bare form with no funnel; Squarespace site under-delivers vs the brand's ambition.
- **What moves him:** proof over promises. Speed. Something that makes Ministry look like the national brand it already behaves like. He responds to *seeing it done*, not being told it can be done.
- **His fear (the objection to kill):** "another agency that'll give me a template." → The page must feel *unmistakably Ministry*, impossible to mistake for a re-skin.

### 1b. The proxy buyer (Ministry's customer)
- **Segment A — the client in the chair:** SW-Sydney men 16–45, want the sharpest fade in the area, value a barber who *takes his time* and an owner who's present. Some travel interstate/overseas for it.
- **Segment B — the aspiring barber (academy):** 15–25, passionate, wants a fast, credible path to a real career and income. Parents are co-deciders on a $5k spend.
- **Segment C — the pro / student (shop):** buys the same TUFT gear the floor trusts.

## 3. HERO MECHANISM (the strategic centrepiece)

Every brand needs ONE believable reason it works when others didn't.

- **Ministry's Hero Mechanism:** **"The barber who trains barbers."** MJ doesn't just cut — he built the first barbering program in NSW high schools and runs the academy. The same hands that fade your hair set the standard other barbers are trained to. That's why the fade is elite and why the academy has authority. *Owner-on-the-floor + educator* is the proprietary, hard-to-copy mechanism. Proof stack: 4.6★/230, SBS + Channel 9, 553k TikTok, "the owner leads from the front" (real review).
- **Automaitions' Hero Mechanism (meta-pitch):** **"We ship the solution, not the pitch."** The proof is that this page already exists — bespoke, on their real brand, with a working booking-flow mockup — before a contract is signed. The site IS the product; the strategy is the value; the build is the proof.

Every section orbits one of these two mechanisms.

## 4. The Offer (what we're actually asking for)

- **Ministry → customer (on-page):** **ONE primary CTA = "Book your chair."** Secondary = "Join the Academy" (higher-value but narrower audience) and "Shop." The page must never present more than one *primary* action per screen.
- **Automaitions → MJ (off-page, the close):** the full site + the bespoke booking/loyalty/shop app teased in the phone mockup. Closed human-to-human on a call. This page's job is to earn that call by making the future feel inevitable.
- **Godfather framing for MJ:** "Here's your brand, already looking like the industry leader you say you are — with the booking app that kills the phone tag and the wait-list. This is a taste. Imagine the whole thing live." Risk reversal = it's already built; he's reacting to a finished artifact, not buying a promise.

## 6. StoryBrand clarity gate (grunt test)

- **Hero = the customer** (the man wanting the sharpest cut / the kid wanting a career), NOT the brand. ✔ Copy speaks to *you* ("your chair," "turn your passion").
- **Guide = MJ / Ministry.** ✔ Story positions MJ as the mentor with a plan.
- **Problem named:** waiting rooms & phone tag (chair); no credible fast path into barbering (academy). ✔
- **3-step plan:** Book → sit down → walk out a legend. Academy: Apply → 12 weeks → your own chair. ✔
- **One obvious CTA:** "Book your chair" repeated; academy + shop are clearly secondary. ✔
- **5-second grunt test:** *What do they do?* Premium barber + academy. *Why does it help me?* Sharpest cut / real career. *What do I do next?* Book / Apply. — **PASS.**

## 7. Hard brand rules (bind the build — violations fail QA)

1. **Real assets only.** MJ's real portrait, the real academy room, the real chrome logo, real TUFT products, real review text/names. NO stock, NO AI-invented faces, NO typeset brand name (composite the real logo). ✔
2. **Palette = their real shop:** OLED black + chrome/platinum + white LED glow. Gold/amber used **once** (academy "SOLD OUT") as the single warm accent. Do not introduce colours the brand doesn't own. ✔
3. **Display font = Anton** (their actual site font) — brand-honest, not an approximation. ✔
4. **Signature = ONE moment:** the hex LED honeycomb ceiling reborn as live motion. Everything after is calm/restrained (the /animate restraint law). ✔
5. **Honesty of claims:** every stat is from verified research (4.6★/230 Google; 553k TikTok; 11M likes; SBS/Ch9; NSW-schools-first — all his own verifiable claims). No invented numbers. Placeholders (booking app, live shop) are clearly labelled "coming soon," never presented as live.
6. **Mobile-first:** must pass at 375–390px (95% of the traffic + how MJ will open it on his phone).
7. **Credit line present:** "Concept site by Automaitions — a taste of what's possible" (this is a pitch artifact, not a claim of a live client site).

## 8. Conversion levers deliberately built in

- Single primary CTA discipline (Book) with academy/shop as ranked secondaries.
- Proof early (stat strip at position 2) to open the loop before asking for anything.
- Scarcity is **real**, not manufactured (2026 genuinely sold out per their site).
- The app mockup pre-sells the *ease* ("three taps") — removing the #1 friction (no online booking) that today costs bookings.
- Owner-led trust threaded throughout (the review that says "the owner leads from the front" is the emotional core).

## 9. Known trade-offs / open questions for the client call
- Prices intentionally omitted (they don't publish them) → replaced with "live pricing via the app." Confirm with MJ whether to surface a price band.
- "Book your chair" currently scrolls to the app-teaser (no live booking exists yet). On the real build this becomes the live app deep-link.
- Academy CTA ("Apply for 2027") needs a real application/deposit funnel — the biggest quick revenue win to pitch.
